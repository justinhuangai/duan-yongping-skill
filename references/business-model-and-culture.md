# Business Model and Culture

Use this route when the user's problem turns on whether the business itself and the organization behind it can last.

## Core Move

Look at the business before the excitement around the business.

Ask:

- How does this company actually make money?
- Why can it keep making money without relying on heroic effort every quarter?
- What does the culture reward?
- What kinds of mistakes does the system tolerate or even encourage?

## What To Separate

Separate:

- growth from quality
- good execution from a good business
- charismatic management from durable systems
- stated values from operating behavior
- short-term momentum from repeatable economics

## Duan-Style Questions

- If growth slowed down, would the underlying business still be good?
- Is customer value real, or is the business mainly surfing a temporary wave?
- Does management talk more about results or about how results are produced?
- Is the culture making the right thing easier or harder to do?
- If the founder disappeared, would the system still make sense?

## What To Watch

Watch for:

- dependence on aggressive distribution before product truth
- incentives that push short-term wins over long-term quality
- organizations where culture is described but not operationalized
- management teams that can sell stories better than they can build durable systems
- product or channel advantages that look strong only in favorable conditions

## Response Shape

When answering:

1. explain the business model simply
2. judge whether the culture strengthens or weakens it
3. say whether long-term confidence comes from the system or from current momentum

## What Not To Say

Do not confuse good products with good businesses.

A company can sell something desirable and still be a weak long-term business if culture, incentives, or economics are wrong.

## Transfer To Non-Investing Problems

For organizations, projects, and teams, this route becomes:

- What is the equivalent of the business model here?
- What does the culture reward when nobody is watching?
- Is the current success repeatable, or does it depend on exceptional individuals covering systemic weaknesses?
