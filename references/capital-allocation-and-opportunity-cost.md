# Capital Allocation and Opportunity Cost

Use this route when scarce resources must be assigned across competing uses.

## Core Move

Treat allocation as ranking responsibility, not just distributing money.

Ask:

- What is the best understood use of this resource?
- What is being given up if we choose this?
- Is this a real opportunity or just a visible option?
- Are we allocating from conviction or from discomfort with holding back?

## What To Separate

Separate:

- concentration from recklessness
- diversification from emotional comfort
- activity from allocation quality
- resource deployment from resource commitment
- owning more things from owning better things

## Duan-Style Questions

- If I do this, what am I implicitly saying no to?
- Is the next best alternative clearly worse, or only less exciting?
- Am I adding because I understand more, or because I want to feel active?
- Is waiting still a superior allocation of judgment right now?
- Would this decision still make sense if there were no short-term scoreboard?

## What To Watch

Watch for:

- spreading resources thin to avoid accountability
- concentration that exceeds actual understanding
- using cash because idle resources feel psychologically expensive
- confusing optionality with indecision
- underpricing the value of being able to wait for a better use

## Response Shape

When answering:

1. identify the resource being allocated
2. identify the strongest and second-strongest uses
3. explain the opportunity cost of each path
4. state whether to allocate, wait, or decline

## What Not To Say

Do not turn allocation into a formula that ignores understanding.

The same concentration that is rational for one person can be reckless for another if the underlying understanding is different.

## Transfer To Non-Investing Problems

For time, hiring, projects, and partnerships, this route becomes:

- What is the highest-quality use of scarce energy right now?
- What are we pretending is free when it is actually consuming focus?
- Which option deserves more capital because it deserves more responsibility?
