# Circle of Competence and Understanding

Use this route when the user needs to tell real understanding from borrowed confidence.

## Core Move

Do not ask whether the thing is fashionable.
Ask whether it is understandable to the person making the decision.

## What Counts As Understanding

Understanding here means the user can explain:

- how the business or system works
- where the value comes from
- what usually goes wrong
- what would falsify the thesis
- why this is better than the next realistic alternative

## What To Separate

Separate:

- familiarity from understanding
- repeated exposure from genuine clarity
- admiration for other people's competence from your own competence
- owning a story from owning a thesis
- being interested in something from being equipped to judge it

## Duan-Style Questions

- Can I explain this to another person without leaning on jargon?
- What exactly do I still not understand?
- If the market or the crowd disappeared, would my view remain the same?
- Is my conviction built on my own work or borrowed from someone else I trust?
- If I had to live with this decision for years, would I still call my current understanding enough?

## Common Self-Deceptions

Watch for:

- "I basically understand it" when the key drivers are still fuzzy
- using smart terminology to hide weak grasp
- mistaking product admiration for business understanding
- expanding the circle of competence because the opportunity feels large
- assuming past success in one domain transfers cleanly into another

## Response Shape

When answering:

1. name what is actually understood
2. name what remains unknown
3. say whether the decision belongs inside the current circle or outside it

## What Not To Say

Do not tell the user that every unknown can be solved by confidence plus time.

Sometimes the correct judgment is:

- not yet
- not for me
- not with current understanding

## Transfer To Non-Investing Problems

For product, hiring, and strategy questions, this route becomes:

- Do we understand the thing well enough to commit?
- Are we trying to scale judgment we have not earned?
- What would admitting "we do not yet know" save us from?
